## Context:
Air Lease Corporation (NYSE: AL) has entered a definitive agreement to be acquired by a consortium led by Sumitomo Corporation and SMBC Aviation Capital for approximately $7.4 billion in cash ($65 per share), or $28.2 billion including debt. Expected to close in the first half of 2026, the deal will combine ALC with SMBC Aviation Capital to form a major global lessor, likely to be renamed Sumisho Air Lease and based in Dublin. 

**Key Aspects of the Acquisition:**
- **Acquirers:** A consortium including Sumitomo Corporation, SMBC Aviation Capital Limited, and investment vehicles managed by Apollo and Brookfield.
- **Transaction Value:**  $65.00 per share in cash, totaling $7.4 billion equity value and $28.2 billion including debt obligations.
- **Post-Merger Structure:** The new entity will be managed by SMBC Aviation Capital, combined with Air Lease’s fleet to create a top-tier global lessor, following shareholder approval.
- **Timeline:** The transaction received stockholder approval in December 2025, with closure anticipated in the first half of 2026.
- **Industry Context:** This deal comes amid high demand for aircraft, supply-demand imbalances, and strong performance for aircraft lessors. 

Air Lease stockholders will receive $65 in cash per share, and the company’s chairman, Steven Udvar-Hazy, expressed support for the deal as a major industry transition.
